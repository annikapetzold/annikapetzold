3 Phase Construction and Renovation

The Hemp House as interim supermarket provides basic staples

Community Building Workshop

Villagers learn construction techniques they can apply to homeprojects

Rural Connection

The Hemp House acts as a community space

When the village's only supermarket gets demolished, its least mobile residents are left dependent on taxi services for basic food supply. Rather than accepting this as a given, the project asks how architecture can solve what logistics cannot. The answer was confirmed in direct conversation with the mayor: an interim market, covering essential supply while the original undergoes a full hemp renovation. The interim structure itself is built from hemp, and built by the community. The local garden association leads the construction, turning the building process into a hands-on workshop transferring knowledge the mayor explicitly identified as desired: how to build for yourself, your garden, your home. Once the supermarket reopens, the structure stays. Inside café, flexible furniture, stored tools, and an activated outdoor space make it a permanent community hub open for construction workshops, reading evenings, and whatever the village needs next. Even though the building was demolished in real life, this project acts as a guideline for the next village by providing municipalities with new perspectives for future construction projects.
