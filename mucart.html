Art Containers

Spaces with controllable conditions for art display and presentations

Located between the informal container art landscape of Bahnwärter Thiel and the established cultural institution of the Volkstheater, MucArt sits at the intersection of two worlds: experimental and formal, spontaneous and structured. The building reflects its dual context in form: a calm, ordered grid mirrors the formal presence of the Volkstheater, housing the school's learning and rehearsal spaces. Container volumes emerge in the atrium, borrowing the informal language of Bahnwärter Thiel to create a low-pressure exhibition and performance space. The load-bearing timber structure defines the building's grid and remains exposed throughout. Passive ventilation is driven by a shed roof with clerestory openings that create continuous airflow through thermal buoyancy. Clay brick ceilings absorb heat during the day and release it slowly at night, eliminating the need for mechanical cooling.
